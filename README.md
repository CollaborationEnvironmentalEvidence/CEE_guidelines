**View the guidelines online:**
https://collaborationenvironmentalevidence.github.io/CEE_guidelines/

---

## What are these guidelines?

These guidelines provide best practice for the **planning, conduct, and reporting** of evidence syntheses in environmental management, including:

* systematic reviews
* systematic maps
* meta-analysis and quantitative synthesis
* structured narrative synthesis

They are designed to support researchers, practitioners, and decision-makers in producing evidence that is:

* transparent
* reproducible
* methodologically robust
* relevant for policy and practice

---

## What does “living guidelines” mean?

Traditional guideline documents are static and updated infrequently. In contrast, this project adopts a **living guideline approach**.

A living guideline is:

* continuously updated as methods develop
* responsive to user feedback and emerging challenges
* openly maintained through community contributions

This allows the guidance to evolve alongside advances in evidence synthesis methods, rather than lag behind them.

## Structure of the guidelines

The guidelines are organised into chapters covering the full evidence synthesis process:

* Aims & Scope
* Question formulation
* Searching and study identification
* Screening and eligibility
* Critical appraisal
* Data extraction
* Synthesis (quantitative and narrative)
* Reporting and transparency

## Principles underpinning the guidelines

These guidelines emphasise:

* explicit consideration of **effect size, uncertainty, and heterogeneity**
* transparent and reproducible workflows
* appropriate synthesis methods aligned with the research question and data
* avoidance of practices that obscure or misrepresent evidence

## Versioning

This project follows a **living versioning model**:

* **Major versions (e.g. 5.0 → 6.0)**
  Substantial revisions to structure or core methodological guidance

* **Minor versions (e.g. 5.0 → 5.1)**
  Additions, clarifications, or moderate updates

* **Patch updates (e.g. 5.1.1)**
  Small corrections, wording changes, or fixes

Updates are made incrementally through GitHub and tracked via commits, issues, and pull requests.

Where appropriate, **stable releases** will be tagged and archived to support citation and reproducibility.
